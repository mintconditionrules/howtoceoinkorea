Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2, 2026

This site is operated by a certified business advisor publishing information about founding and running a company in Korea. This policy explains what personal data is collected, why, and how it is handled.

What we collect

The only personal data collected on this site is your email address, and only if you voluntarily subscribe to the newsletter. No other data (name, phone, IP address, cookies) is collected or stored by this site.

Why we collect it

Your email address is used solely to send the Seoul Brief newsletter — occasional emails about doing business in Korea. It will never be sold, rented, or shared with third parties for marketing purposes.

Where it is stored

Submitted email addresses are stored in a private Google Sheets spreadsheet, hosted on Google's servers. Google's own privacy policy applies to that storage. See policies.google.com/privacy.

Your rights

Under Korea's Personal Information Protection Act (개인정보 보호법 / PIPA) and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data held about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data at any time
  • Withdraw consent for email communications

To exercise any of these rights, or to unsubscribe, contact us at the address below and your data will be removed within 7 days.

Cookies & tracking

This site does not use cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics scripts. No third-party advertising or profiling takes place.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the "Last updated" date above will be revised. Continued use of the site after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact
For data requests, corrections, or unsubscribe: reach out via the Contact page. We will respond within 7 business days.
